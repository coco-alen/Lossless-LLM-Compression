\begin{abstract}
Disaggregated prefill-decode serving improves LLM utilization by separating compute-heavy prompt processing from latency-sensitive generation, but it exposes KV-cache transfer as a first-order time-to-first-token bottleneck. We present \splitzip, a GPU-native codec for KV-cache transfer that exploits a stable numerical regularity of BF16 and FP8 KV tensors: their exponent fields occupy a small subset of the representable range. \splitzip stores sign and mantissa bits verbatim, packs frequent exponents into fixed-width codes, and uses an escape stream for rare BF16 exponents to guarantee bitwise exact reconstruction. On controlled H200 microbenchmarks, the BF16 lossless codec reaches $1.333\times$ compression with 252/1460~GB/s encode/decode throughput; on real Qwen3-30B-A3B BF16 KV caches, it obtains $1.293$--$1.301\times$ compression with exact reconstruction and a $1.238\times$ end-to-end Mooncake TCP transfer speedup. The same format-aware idea applies to native FP8 caches, giving an additional $1.19\times$ (E4M3) to $1.39\times$ (E5M2) lossless compression on top of FP8 storage. Across six model settings and 140 generation prompts, \splitzip preserves lossless BF16 outputs exactly and provides a deployable compression layer for disaggregated LLM serving.
\end{abstract}
