\section*{NeurIPS Paper Checklist}

\begin{enumerate}

\item {\bf Claims}
    \item[] Question: Do the main claims made in the abstract and introduction accurately reflect the paper's contributions and scope?
    \item[] Answer: \answerYes{}.
    \item[] Justification: The abstract and introduction distinguish controlled microbenchmarks, real-KV Mooncake measurements, lossless BF16 reconstruction, native FP8 compression, and the limitations of each setting.

\item {\bf Limitations}
    \item[] Question: Does the paper discuss the limitations of the work performed by the authors?
    \item[] Answer: \answerYes{}.
    \item[] Justification: In Section~\ref{sec:conclusion}.

\item {\bf Theory assumptions and proofs}
    \item[] Question: For each theoretical result, does the paper provide the full set of assumptions and a complete proof?
    \item[] Answer: \answerNA{}.
    \item[] Justification: The paper is an empirical systems paper and does not claim new theoretical results beyond the direct bitwise reconstruction property of the lossless codec.

\item {\bf Experimental result reproducibility}
    \item[] Question: Does the paper fully disclose all information needed to reproduce the main experimental results to the extent that it affects the main claims?
    \item[] Answer: \answerYes{}.
    \item[] Justification: The experiments section specifies hardware, models, settings, baselines.

\item {\bf Open access to data and code}
    \item[] Question: Does the paper provide open access to the data and code, with sufficient instructions to faithfully reproduce the main experimental results?
    \item[] Answer: \answerYes{}.
    \item[] Justification: The repository includes the SplitZip implementation and experiment scripts used for profiling, baseline comparisons. It will be open-sourced after the article is accepted.

\item {\bf Experimental setting/details}
    \item[] Question: Does the paper specify all training and test details necessary to understand the results?
    \item[] Answer: \answerYes{}.
    \item[] Justification: No model training is performed. The test setup, evaluated models, context lengths, transport stack, precision modes, and measured quantities are described in Section~\ref{sec:experiments}.

\item {\bf Experiment statistical significance}
    \item[] Question: Does the paper report error bars suitably and correctly defined or other appropriate information about statistical significance?
    \item[] Answer: \answerYes{}.
    \item[] Justification: The encoding and decoding throughput of the method may be influenced by randomness; therefore, multiple measurements were conducted, and the measurement fluctuations are reported.

\item {\bf Experiments compute resources}
    \item[] Question: For each experiment, does the paper provide sufficient information on compute resources needed to reproduce the experiments?
    \item[] Answer: \answerYes{}.
    \item[] Justification: In Section \ref{sec:setup}.

\item {\bf Code of ethics}
    \item[] Question: Does the research conducted in the paper conform, in every respect, with the NeurIPS Code of Ethics?
    \item[] Answer: \answerYes{}.
    \item[] Justification: The work studies compression and systems performance for existing LLM serving workloads and does not involve human subjects, private data collection, or model release.

\item {\bf Broader impacts}
    \item[] Question: Does the paper discuss both potential positive societal impacts and negative societal impacts of the work performed?
    \item[] Answer: \answerYes{}.
    \item[] Justification: In Section~\ref{sec:conclusion}.

\item {\bf Safeguards}
    \item[] Question: Does the paper describe safeguards for responsible release of data or models that have a high risk for misuse?
    \item[] Answer: \answerNA{}.
    \item[] Justification: The paper does not release a new model, dataset, or user-facing generative system; it releases a compression method for KV tensors.

\item {\bf Licenses for existing assets}
    \item[] Question: Are creators or original owners of assets used in the paper properly credited and are licenses and terms of use explicitly mentioned and respected?
    \item[] Answer: \answerYes{}.
    \item[] Justification: The draft cites related baseline and datasets. All authors are listed in shared order..

\item {\bf New assets}
    \item[] Question: Are new assets introduced in the paper well documented and is the documentation provided alongside the assets?
    \item[] Answer: \answerYes{}.
    \item[] Justification: The paper introduces SplitZip and the accompanying implementation. The potentially released repository will document how to use the codec and include scripts for profiling codec throughput, evaluating compression behavior, and reproducing end-to-end KV-cache transfer benchmarks.

\item {\bf Crowdsourcing and research with human subjects}
    \item[] Question: For crowdsourcing experiments and research with human subjects, does the paper include the full text of instructions given to participants and compensation details?
    \item[] Answer: \answerNA{}.
    \item[] Justification: This work does not involve crowdsourcing, user studies, or experiments with human subjects.

\item {\bf Institutional review board approvals or equivalent for research with human subjects}
    \item[] Question: Does the paper describe potential risks to study participants and whether IRB approvals were obtained?
    \item[] Answer: \answerNA{}.
    \item[] Justification: This work does not involve human-subject research, and therefore IRB approval or equivalent review is not applicable.

\item {\bf Declaration of LLM usage}
    \item[] Question: Does the paper describe the usage of LLMs if it is an important, original, or non-standard component of the core methods in this research?
    \item[] Answer: \answerNA{}.
    \item[] Justification: LLMs are used as standard serving workloads for evaluating KV-cache transfer and compression performance. They are not used as an original, non-standard, or decision-making component of the proposed compression method.

\end{enumerate}
