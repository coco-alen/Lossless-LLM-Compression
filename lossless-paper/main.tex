\begin{abstract}
Prefill--decode disaggregation improves the scalability of large language model serving, but introduces a new communication bottleneck: the KV cache generated by prefill workers must be transferred to decode workers before token generation can proceed. 
This bottleneck becomes increasingly severe for long-input workloads, where the KV cache grows with sequence length. 
Existing lossless neural-network compression methods are not well suited to this setting, as they primarily target offline weight compression, rely on CPU-side or variable-length coding, or optimize decompression while leaving online compression too slow for the critical path.
We present SplitZip, a GPU-friendly lossless compression scheme for KV-cache transfer. 
SplitZip exploits the observation that most redundancy in floating-point KV activations lies in the exponent field. 
It encodes the most frequent exponent values with fixed-length codes, and records rare exponents through a compact escape stream. 
A calibrated top-16 exponent codebook enables fast online encoding, while the regular dense path and sparse escape correction make both encoding and decoding efficient on GPUs.
On real BF16 activation tensors, SplitZip achieves 435.3 GB/s compression throughput and 1763.8 GB/s decompression throughput, substantially outperforming prior lossless compressors on the latency-critical codec path. 
End-to-end transfer experiments show up to 1.33$\times$ speedup for BF16 KV-cache transfer under CPU-RDMA and RoCE settings. 
SplitZip also provides additional gains on FP8 KV caches, achieving up to 1.17$\times$ speedup over native FP8 transfer.
\end{abstract}
